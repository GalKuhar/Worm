Halping Quest! (Host)
Author: unsanity
Total length: 7 chapters, 6.4k words (SV, SB)
Created on: 12th May
Updated on: Fri at 5 pm UTC
Tags: host thread
Summary:
The host thread writes the trigger event.

After the character is created in the shard thread, the host thread will create a trigger event. Configure is a shard specialized in creating, tweaking, and adjusting powers, and more often than not will include those abilities in the powers it grants its host. But a strong foundation must still be made before any shenanigans can be built on top, and the trigger event will affect how the power is applied and expressed. It's impossible to precisely determine the outcome of a trigger event (translation: don't try to design the power itself), but clearly defined trigger events often have predictable tendencies. For instance, a trigger defined by social isolation will always result in powers with a strong Master component, and so on. You can even go for a multitrigger if you like. Just keep in mind that, no, you can't have the clusterfuck trigger mindmeld a la Rain O'Fire, and also your cluster mates aren't controllable characters. Aiming for multiple power categories is also a possibility. Second triggers are not something you can actively decide on, although a sufficiently severe trigger event might result in a second trigger.

Halping Quest! (Shard)
Author: unsanity
Total length: 7 chapters, 20k words (SB)
Created on: 12th May
Updated on: Fri at 5 pm UTC
Tags: shard thread
Summary:
The shard thread writes the backstory of the host.

Pretty much anything goes in this case, as long as it isn't physically impossible for the setting. The host must be an unpowered person in the city of Brockton Bay, in Earth Bet, at a time no later than the date Worm starts on. Original characters are allowed, within reason. Entire families can be invented wholesale, and even entire streets, as long as they conform to the vaguely defined geography of Brockton Bay and don't contradict anything that already exists. OC parahumans, on the other hand, aren't allowed; you can use existing parahumans in the unpowered host's backstory, as long as it's reasonable and doesn't contradict canon. The host can belong to whatever organizations they could reasonably access given their location, disposition, resources, age, and so on. Feel free to include details such as lifestyle, personality, hobbies, friendships, career, education, and whatnot. Anything that's important to the character will, more likely than not, have at least a passing importance to the story.
